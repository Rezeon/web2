Definition and Meaning

Wayang is a performing art that uses puppets or shadows to tell stories. The puppets are projected onto a white screen or cloth, known as a puppet or kelir screen, using lights positioned behind the screen. Stories in puppet shows are often taken from epics or folktales that have deep moral, religious, and cultural values.

Cultural Function and Meaning

Wayang is not just entertainment, but also has a deep cultural meaning. Puppet performances are often used as a means to teach the values of life, morality, and history to the audience. In addition, puppets are also often used in religious rituals or certain traditional ceremonies in Indonesia.
